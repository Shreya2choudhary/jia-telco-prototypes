Use case 3 — Call Intelligence

Jia answers the calls you can't

When a call comes in and you don't pick up, Jia steps in on your behalf — takes the message, surfaces it as a live transcript, and lets you listen in, join, or decline at any time.

3A · Known caller — you don't answer in 5 rings
Scenario: Ananya (saved contact) calls. The phone rings 5 times — your default ring count, configurable. You don't pick up, so Jia auto-answers, asks why she's calling, takes the message about a birthday dinner, and pushes a summary card you can act on.
3A.1 · Call + Messages

RCS notification

Mid-call, Jia drops an RCS banner with a live transcript. Tap it to see Jia and Ananya's bubbles streaming in real time, with three chips: Listen in, Join the call, or Decline.

3A.2 · Call + MyJio

MyJio in-call panel

Same flow, surfaced inside the MyJio app instead of RCS. Live transcript with branded bubbles, the same three actions, and a MyJio-styled summary card at the end.

3B · Unknown caller — Jia screens before the phone rings
Scenario: An unknown number calls — Rohit from HDFC Bank pitching a pre-approved credit card. Phone never rings; Jia intercepts at the network and answers directly. A notification slides down silently with a live transcript. The user reads two exchanges, recognises it as a sales call, taps Decline. Jia closes politely, summary card flags the number as spam.
3B.1 · Screen + Messages

RCS notification

Notification banner appears at the top of the lock screen. Tap it to open the live RCS transcript — Jia and Rohit's bubbles streaming, with three chips: Listen in, Take the call, or Decline.

MyJio in-call panel
